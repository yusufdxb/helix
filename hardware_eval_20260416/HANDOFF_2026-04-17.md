## What Phase 3 proved / refuted in one paragraph

Over 60 min with all 6 lifecycle nodes `active [3]`, the HELIX adapter's total RSS grew from 257 MB → 282 MB and *plateaued* by t ≈ 38 min (growth rate decelerated 7 × between first and second half-period). Phase 2's runaway-leak worry was a pre-plateau rising-curve artifact, not a leak. Per-node analysis further refuted the Phase 2 attribution: `topic_rate_monitor` had the *smallest* RSS growth (+2.48 MB) despite dominating CPU. Session 1 benchmark numbers reproduced across 11 days within ±2 %. Most actionable finding: the 250 Hz `/utlidar/imu` rate-monitor callback alone accounts for ~94 % of topic_rate_monitor's CPU and ~88 % of the whole adapter. Dropping it from `topics` takes adapter CPU from 48 % → 6 % of one core with no effect on the other fault-detection paths.
